Night Has Come is a K-drama, a Korean drama. This K-drama is about high school students who are forced to play a mafia game at a youth center they visit for a school trip. An app is installed on everyone's phone, and the app identifies everyone. There are 4 types of identities: mafia, doctor, police, and civilian. The mafia's job is to kill one of the civilians when night falls. The doctor's ability is to heal someone they choose. The police's ability is to learn the identity of someone they choose. The civilians' task is to find the mafia and win the game by surviving. However, there are rules to the game. First, players must play the game while remaining within a specific area. Players must vote for someone within a given time. The mafias must kill the person they have chosen among themselves within a certain time; they cannot kill without voting. One mafia member cannot kill another, and cannot reveal the identity of another mafia member. Those who break these rules are executed. And the person with the most votes is also executed. Surviving under these conditions is very difficult. But at the end of the game, a mafia member who sacrificed himself for his friend and who was actually dead before but had taken on the character he designed, commits suicide. But his friend also fails to win the game and dies. Only one civilian remains, and no one else is left. At the last moment, the girl who was the civilian who died opens her eyes in a strange place. It turns out that this game wasn't actually happening in reality; the girl, who was a mafia member, committed suicide in the real world because of her friends, and her parents imprisoned everyone in the class in an endless game to take revenge. At the end of the series... The game starts again. So what he wants to convey here is that the series ends badly because they are trapped in an endless game.
